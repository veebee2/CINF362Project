Best Invasive Removal Practices

Manual Extraction

For species like Garlic Mustard, hand-pulling before seeds drop is the most effective way to clear small areas without chemicals.

Solarization

Covering cleared soil with black plastic tarps during the summer months uses heat to kill off remaining invasive root fragments and seeds.

Native Pollinator Gardens

The final step in conservation is replacement. Once invasives are gone, planting NY-native flowers ensures that pollinators have a stable food source.

  • Step 1: Clear the site of all non-native debris.
  • Step 2: Test your soil for pH and moisture levels.
  • Step 3: Select NY-native species that bloom in succession from April to October.
