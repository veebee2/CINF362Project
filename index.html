Why This Site Matters

Native plants are essential to the health of New York State's ecosystems. They support pollinators, improve soil health, and help maintain biodiversity. This site provides clear, accessible information to help you understand and support native plant conservation in your everyday life.

New York is home to over 3,000 native plant species, ranging from the towering white pines of the Adirondacks to the delicate spring wildflowers of the Hudson Valley. These plants have shaped our landscapes for thousands of years and form the foundation of the food web that supports nearly all wildlife in the state.

Why Native Plants Are Important

Native plants have evolved alongside local wildlife, making them critical for pollinators like bees and butterflies. They require less maintenance, use less water, and are more resilient to local climate conditions compared to non-native species.

Research shows that native plants support far more wildlife than non-native ornamentals. A single native oak tree can host more than 500 species of caterpillars, which in turn feed nesting songbirds. A non-native ginkgo tree, by comparison, supports only about 5 species. By choosing native plants, you are not just adding beauty to your yard, you are rebuilding the ecological web that local wildlife depends on.

Native plants also help fight climate change. Their deep root systems store carbon, prevent soil erosion, and improve water filtration. Many native species can survive on rainfall alone once established, eliminating the need for the irrigation and chemical inputs that traditional lawns demand.

What You Can Do

Whether you're a homeowner, gardener, or student, you can help by planting native species, removing invasive plants, and spreading awareness in your community.

Even small actions add up. Replacing just a few square feet of lawn with native flowers can create a meaningful refuge for monarch butterflies and native bees. Skipping pesticide use, leaving leaf litter in garden beds over winter, and buying plants from native plant nurseries are all simple changes that make a real difference for the local environment.
